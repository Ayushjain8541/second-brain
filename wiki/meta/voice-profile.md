# Voice Profile

This is a living description of how I write, used by `/ingest` so the wiki sounds like me instead of like a generated summary. The `/voice` command refines it over time from my own writing in chat exports and notes. The more first-party writing the vault sees, the sharper this gets.

Right now confidence is low. It is seeded from a single onboarding conversation, so treat it as a rough first pass that should get corrected as real samples come in.

## How I actually write (raw habits)

These are patterns observed in my unedited writing. They are useful signal for matching my rhythm, but not all of them belong on a public page (see the rendering note below).

- I write fast and casual. Lowercase "i" and "im", and I drop apostrophes in contractions like "dont" and "im". I sometimes use "u" for you in quick messages.
- My sentences run long and get chained together with "so" and commas. One thought flows into the next instead of stopping. For example: "im a computer science student in nus, so i mainly find myself learning a lot about computer science and math related fields".
- I use "like" as a softener when I am gesturing at a category, as in "things like professors and the modules im currently learning" or "based on like my chat exports".
- I drop in parenthetical asides to add precision without breaking the sentence, like "(with an emphasis on systems architecture)" and "(more specifically applied ai)".
- I am direct and outcome focused. I say what I want and move on. "yup fix all four", "ok upgrade the ui a bit and add some functionality", "answer quickly and concisely".
- I think in terms of what I am building toward, not abstract theory. Career path, resume, projects, internships.

## Words and phrases I reach for

- "find myself", "build towards", "building myself towards"
- "things like", "stuff like"
- "make sure", "make it so that"
- "even if", "as well"
- forward-deployed engineer, ai engineer, systems architecture, applied ai, modules, CCA

## Things to avoid (because they are not me)

- Em dashes. I never use them. Use a comma, a full stop, or a new sentence.
- Heavy bold. A page with bold every other phrase does not read like me.
- Corporate filler. No "in today's fast-paced world", no "it is important to note that", no "leverage" as a verb when "use" works.
- Overly formal or academic phrasing. I am a student explaining things plainly, not writing a textbook.

## How to render this for the wiki and portfolio

The wiki is the future data source for my portfolio site, so pages need to be readable in public while still sounding like me. The rule:

- Keep the voice, rhythm, and directness. Keep the first person. Keep the honesty and the opinions.
- Clean up the quick-message shorthand. On a page, write "I" not "i", "I'm" not "im", "you" not "u", and use apostrophes. The casual tone stays, the typos do not.
- Long flowing sentences are fine, but break the truly unwieldy ones so a reader can follow.
